02 - Articles

Build - 4 min read

Why I prefer shipping before I feel ready.

Waiting until a project feels perfect usually means I am judging it in the wrong place. The real test is not the local folder, the Notion plan or the mockup. The real test is whether someone can use it.

  • Build the smallest useful version, then let real usage expose what matters.
  • Keep the scope honest, because a finished small product teaches more than a huge unfinished one.
  • Improve in public, so every release becomes part of the learning loop.

AI Tools - 5 min read

How I use AI without outsourcing my taste.

AI helps me move faster when I already know what I am trying to make. It is strongest when I use it to unblock details, compare options and turn rough ideas into something I can inspect.

  • I keep the direction human: product, style and final calls are still my responsibility.
  • I ask for concrete output: code, variants, checks, tradeoffs and missing edge cases.
  • I review the result, because speed without taste just creates more cleanup.

Study - 3 min read

Being a CS student and an indie hacker at the same time.

Computer Science gives me deeper tools for thinking. Indie hacking gives me pressure to turn those tools into something people can actually open, test and judge.

  • The theory helps when projects need structure, data, systems thinking and debugging discipline.
  • The products help when theory needs constraints, deadlines and real users.
  • The balance is the point: study deep, build small, repeat often.
